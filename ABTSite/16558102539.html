Configurator

Program blocks are stored in the library without I/Os. Use the auto-create and auto-connect functions to create I/Os and/or to connect them to the interface blocks, e.g., R_A, CMD_B. Alternatively, take the I/Os from the folder containing the predefined I/Os in the library and/or from the plant and connect them to the interface blocks.

Function

CenOpMod21 distributes central commands and operating modes to the connected room automation areas via group mechanism. The commands may originate from a scheduler or a primary operating unit.

The most important functions in the program block are:

  • Central operating mode command evaluation
    Possible sources include:
    • Scheduler
    • Manual operating mode selection
  • Night cooling and/or free cooling request
  • Warm-up and/or cool-down request (from optimal start/stop functionality)
  • Manual operation of an air handling unit
  • Central condition trigger

Further options

Description

Anti-glare protection prolongation

The options in "Operating mode extended" can overwrite (e.g., according to the central scheduler) the settings for presence mode or operating strategy in the room.

For example, the setting in the rooms for "Lighting presence mode" is "Consider present&absent" for the room operating mode "Economy". At night (e.g., from 10 p.m. to 5 a.m.), this may not be desired, because security personnel are moving through the building and have their own flashlights and do not want the lights to come on and show where they are. In this case, the central function sends "None" to "Lighting presence mode" at that time (e.g., from 10 p.m. to 5 a.m.).

The option "Anti-glare protection prolongation" calculates the sunrise and sunset times, and adds some time before sunrise and after sunset and on a weekday, and overwrites the "Shading operating strategy" for the room operating mode "Economy" to "Anti-glare protection".

Further information

Central operating mode command evaluation

The central operating mode command is run with priority 15, if it is coming out of a scheduler, or with priority 8, if the manual operating mode selection is active.

Night cooling, free cooling, warm-up and cool-down requests

CenOpMod21 can receive night cooling, free cooling, warm-up and/or cool-down requests (or calculate warm-up and/or cool-down requests with its own optional OSSC logic) from the air handling unit or from other logic and transfer them to the room automation. The plant operating mode in the area is switched if area conditions permit.

Manual central air handler mode

In addition, the plant operating mode of the area can be switched to Comfort via the manual air handler mode at priority 7. For that, the OpModMan or OpModSwi from the air handler must be connected to the corresponding interface (Option: Forward manual air handling unit operating mode).

Central condition trigger

CenOpMod21 can distribute a central condition trigger (1: None, 2: Energy efficiency condition, 3: Comfort condition) to the room automation areas. Depending on the trigger type, the manual operation is reset due to the sent trigger. For example, sending an energy efficiency trigger resets the manually set area temperature setpoints and the lighting and shading manual operation depending on how the parameterization is set in the area.

Room temperature collection

CenOpMod21 can collect and evaluate the room temperatures from the room automation via grouping mechanism.

Delayed operation

There are three more group managers for delayed operation. The goal is to distribute the rooms to more group managers and then make sure that not all rooms change their operating mode at the same time.

CenOpMod21 belongs to the building scope and can be used in two ways:

  • For commanding subordinate central functions in the floor scope.
  • For commanding subordinate room controllers in the room scope.
